## Core Books

### The DevOps Handbook: How to Create World-Class Agility, Reliability, and Security in Technology Organizations
- **Authors:** Gene Kim, Jez Humble, Patrick Debois, John Willis
- **Publisher:** IT Revolution Press, 2016
- **ISBN:** 978-1942788003
- **Why it matters:** The comprehensive reference for DevOps culture and practice. Covers the Three Ways (Flow, Feedback, Continual Learning), value stream mapping, automated testing, deployment pipelines, telemetry, and organizational transformation. The 2nd edition (2021) adds chapters on security (DevSecOps) and remote work.

### The Phoenix Project: A Novel About IT, DevOps, and Helping Your Business Win
- **Authors:** Gene Kim, Kevin Behr, George Spafford
- **Publisher:** IT Revolution Press, 2013
- **ISBN:** 978-0988262508
- **Why it matters:** Business novel that makes DevOps concepts accessible by dramatizing real problems: unplanned work, poor change management, siloed teams, and how applying Theory of Constraints and Lean principles transforms IT delivery. Widely used to build organizational buy-in for DevOps transformation.

### Continuous Delivery: Reliable Software Releases through Build, Test, and Deployment Automation
- **Authors:** Jez Humble, David Farley
- **Publisher:** Addison-Wesley, 2010
- **ISBN:** 978-0321601919
- **Why it matters:** The foundational text for the deployment pipeline. Covers build and deployment scripting, test automation strategies (unit, integration, acceptance, exploratory), release strategies (blue/green, canary, feature flags), infrastructure and environment management. Still the definitive reference despite its age.

### Site Reliability Engineering: How Google Runs Production Systems
- **Editors:** Betsy Beyer, Chris Jones, Jennifer Petoff, Niall Richard Murphy
- **Publisher:** O'Reilly Media, 2016
- **ISBN:** 978-1491929124
- **URL:** https://sre.google/sre-book/table-of-contents/ (freely available online)
- **Why it matters:** Defines the SRE discipline: SLIs/SLOs/SLAs, error budgets, toil elimination, automation, monitoring, on-call practices, incident management, blameless postmortems, and capacity planning. Direct insight into how Google runs production at scale.

### The Site Reliability Workbook: Practical Ways to Implement SRE
- **Editors:** Betsy Beyer, Niall Richard Murphy, David K. Rensin, Kent Kawahara, Stephen Thorne
- **Publisher:** O'Reilly Media, 2018
- **ISBN:** 978-1492029502
- **URL:** https://sre.google/workbook/table-of-contents/ (freely available online)
- **Why it matters:** Companion to the SRE book with concrete implementation guidance, case studies from non-Google companies, and practical examples of SLO implementation, alerting strategy, and on-call design.

### Accelerate: The Science of Lean Software and DevOps
- **Authors:** Nicole Forsgren, Jez Humble, Gene Kim
- **Publisher:** IT Revolution Press, 2018
- **ISBN:** 978-1942788331
- **Why it matters:** Research-backed evidence (4-year DORA survey of 23,000+ technology professionals) that specific technical practices — continuous delivery, trunk-based development, test automation, loosely coupled architecture — measurably improve both IT and organizational performance. The scientific foundation for DevOps investment decisions.

### Docker Deep Dive
- **Author:** Nigel Poulton
- **Publisher:** Leanpub / self-published (regularly updated)
- **Why it matters:** Practical, up-to-date guide to Docker. Covers images, containers, volumes, networking, Docker Compose, and multi-stage builds. Updated to track current Docker versions.

### Modern Software Engineering: Doing What Works to Build Better Software Faster
- **Author:** David Farley
- **Publisher:** Addison-Wesley, 2022
- **ISBN:** 978-0137314911
- **Why it matters:** Argues for treating software development as an engineering discipline with empirical feedback, iteration, and modularity. Complements continuous delivery with a broader philosophy.

## Influential Articles and Reports

### "Lessons Learned from 20 Years of SRE" — Google Engineering Blog
- **URL:** https://sre.google/
- Historical evolution of SRE practices from Google's perspective.

### DORA State of DevOps Report (Annual)
- **Publisher:** DORA (now part of Google Cloud)
- **URL:** https://dora.dev/research/
- **Why it matters:** Annual survey-based research identifying practices that predict elite DevOps performance. Consistently identifies continuous delivery, trunk-based development, and psychological safety as differentiators.

### "12-Factor App" Methodology
- **Author:** Adam Wiggins (Heroku co-founder)
- **URL:** https://12factor.net/
- **Why it matters:** 12 methodology guidelines for building scalable, maintainable, cloud-native applications. Covers codebase, dependencies, config, backing services, build/release/run, processes, port binding, concurrency, disposability, dev/prod parity, logs, and admin processes. The foundation of cloud-native application design.
