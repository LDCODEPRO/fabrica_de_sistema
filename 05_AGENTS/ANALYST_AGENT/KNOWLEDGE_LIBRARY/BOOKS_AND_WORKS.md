# BOOKS AND WORKS — ANALYST AGENT

## Core Books

### Software Requirements (3rd Edition)
**Authors**: Karl Wiegers & Joy Beatty  
**Year**: 2013  
**Publisher**: Microsoft Press  
**Summary**: The most comprehensive reference on software requirements engineering. Covers the full requirements lifecycle: elicitation, analysis, specification, verification, and management. Includes templates, checklists, and case studies. The standard reference for requirements practitioners.  
**Key Concepts**: Requirements types, quality attributes, requirements traceability, change management, use cases, user stories, context diagrams.

---

### Requirements by Collaboration: Workshops for Defining Needs
**Author**: Ellen Gottesdiener  
**Year**: 2002  
**Publisher**: Addison-Wesley  
**Summary**: Provides a structured methodology for running requirements workshops, including facilitation techniques, workshop design, and conflict resolution. Shifts requirements work from document-driven to conversation-driven.  
**Key Concepts**: JAD (Joint Application Design) workshops, facilitation roles, requirements models, stakeholder participation.

---

### Writing Effective Use Cases
**Author**: Alistair Cockburn  
**Year**: 2000  
**Publisher**: Addison-Wesley  
**Summary**: The definitive guide to use case writing. Defines goal levels, actor types, preconditions, triggers, success scenarios, and extension scenarios. Provides a precise notation and a pragmatic approach to choosing the right level of formality.  
**Key Concepts**: Goal levels (cloud, sea, fish), casual vs. fully-dressed use cases, extension scenarios, actor-goal table, use case survey.

---

### User Stories Applied: For Agile Software Development
**Author**: Mike Cohn  
**Year**: 2004  
**Publisher**: Addison-Wesley  
**Summary**: Defines user stories as a requirements technique for agile teams. Covers story writing, acceptance test definition, story splitting, and backlog management. Introduces the INVEST criteria as a quality check for individual stories.  
**Key Concepts**: INVEST, acceptance criteria, story splitting, story maps, release planning with stories.

---

### Specification by Example: How Successful Teams Deliver the Right Software
**Author**: Gojko Adzic  
**Year**: 2011  
**Publisher**: Manning Publications  
**Summary**: Based on interviews with 50 teams, describes how leading agile teams use concrete examples to specify, develop, and test software. Introduces the pattern of "living documentation" and shows how to implement it with tools like Cucumber and FitNesse.  
**Key Concepts**: Specification by Example, Given/When/Then, living documentation, executable specifications, key examples.

---

### Introducing EventStorming
**Author**: Alberto Brandolini  
**Year**: 2021  
**Publisher**: Leanpub  
**Summary**: The authoritative guide to Event Storming, a workshop format for collaborative domain exploration. Shows how to use domain events, commands, aggregates, and policies to map complex business domains without formal notation.  
**Key Concepts**: Domain events (orange), commands (blue), aggregates (yellow), policies (purple), read models (green), hot spots.

---

### BABOK Guide v3 — A Guide to the Business Analysis Body of Knowledge
**Author**: International Institute of Business Analysis (IIBA)  
**Year**: 2015  
**Publisher**: IIBA  
**Summary**: The global standard for business analysis. Defines six knowledge areas, 50 techniques, and the competencies required of business analysts. Used as the basis for CBAP certification.  
**Key Concepts**: Business Analysis Planning and Monitoring, Elicitation and Collaboration, Requirements Life Cycle Management, Strategy Analysis, Solution Evaluation.

---

### IEEE 830-1998: Recommended Practice for Software Requirements Specifications
**Author**: IEEE Computer Society  
**Year**: 1998 (superseded by ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148:2018)  
**Publisher**: IEEE  
**Summary**: The foundational standard for structuring Software Requirements Specifications (SRS). Defines sections, quality attributes, and formatting conventions for formal requirements documents.  
**Key Concepts**: SRS structure, completeness, consistency, traceability, verifiability, functional and non-functional requirements sections.
