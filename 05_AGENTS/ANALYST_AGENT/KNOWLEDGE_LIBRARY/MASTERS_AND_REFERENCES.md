# MASTERS AND REFERENCES — ANALYST AGENT

## Karl Wiegers
**Domain**: Software requirements engineering  
**Key Contributions**:
- Wrote the most widely used textbook on software requirements (*Software Requirements*, now in its 3rd edition).
- Defined the taxonomy of functional requirements, non-functional requirements, constraints, and business rules.
- Developed a comprehensive requirements process including elicitation, analysis, specification, and validation.
- Introduced the concept of requirements quality attributes: complete, correct, feasible, necessary, prioritized, unambiguous, verifiable.

**Primary Work**: *Software Requirements* (3rd ed., 2013, with Joy Beatty)  
**Relevance to Analyst**: Foundation for all requirements documentation, quality criteria, and review processes.

---

## Ellen Gottesdiener
**Domain**: Collaborative requirements, product discovery  
**Key Contributions**:
- Pioneered collaborative requirements workshops as a structured elicitation technique.
- Developed the Requirements by Collaboration method, emphasizing facilitated workshops over document-heavy approaches.
- Introduced the concept of "product options" for structured backlog management and discovery.

**Primary Works**: *Requirements by Collaboration* (2002), *The Software Requirements Memory Jogger* (2005)  
**Relevance to Analyst**: Workshop facilitation techniques, collaborative elicitation, structured discovery sessions.

---

## Alistair Cockburn
**Domain**: Use cases, agile methods, software design  
**Key Contributions**:
- Defined a rigorous, practical format for writing use cases including goal levels (sea level, cloud, kite) and actor-goal relationship mapping.
- Introduced the distinction between casual and fully-dressed use cases for different formality needs.
- Developed the Crystal family of agile methodologies.

**Primary Work**: *Writing Effective Use Cases* (2000)  
**Relevance to Analyst**: Definitive reference for use case structure, actor identification, and scenario writing.

---

## Mike Cohn
**Domain**: Agile user stories, backlog management  
**Key Contributions**:
- Defined the three-part user story format and the INVEST criteria.
- Documented story splitting patterns for large stories that cannot fit a sprint.
- Showed how acceptance tests define the boundaries of a story better than prose descriptions.

**Primary Work**: *User Stories Applied: For Agile Software Development* (2004)  
**Relevance to Analyst**: User story writing, acceptance criteria definition, backlog decomposition.

---

## Gojko Adzic
**Domain**: Specification by Example, BDD, acceptance testing  
**Key Contributions**:
- Developed the Specification by Example approach, which uses concrete examples to define and verify behavior.
- Showed how executable specifications (Gherkin/Cucumber) bridge the gap between business and technical stakeholders.
- Introduced the concept of "living documentation" — specifications that are also automated tests.

**Primary Work**: *Specification by Example: How Successful Teams Deliver the Right Software* (2011)  
**Relevance to Analyst**: BDD-style acceptance criteria, Given/When/Then format, collaborative specification workshops.

---

## Alberto Brandolini
**Domain**: Event Storming, domain-driven design  
**Key Contributions**:
- Created Event Storming as a workshop format for collaborative domain exploration.
- Showed how domain events, commands, aggregates, and policies can be discovered in a collaborative session with no UML required.
- Applied DDD concepts to requirements discovery in a visual, accessible way.

**Primary Work**: *Introducing EventStorming* (2021, Leanpub)  
**Relevance to Analyst**: Domain discovery workshops, business process mapping, identifying bounded contexts and business rules.

---

## IIBA (International Institute of Business Analysis)
**Domain**: Business analysis body of knowledge  
**Key Contributions**:
- Published the BABOK (Business Analysis Body of Knowledge), the global standard for business analysis practices.
- Defined knowledge areas: Business Analysis Planning, Elicitation and Collaboration, Requirements Life Cycle Management, Strategy Analysis, Requirements Analysis and Design Definition, Solution Evaluation.
- Provides the CBAP (Certified Business Analysis Professional) certification.

**Primary Work**: *BABOK Guide v3* (2015)  
**Relevance to Analyst**: Comprehensive framework for all business analysis activities and competencies.
