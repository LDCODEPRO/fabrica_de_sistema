# BOOKS AND WORKS — ORCHESTRATOR AGENT

## Core Books

### Kanban: Successful Evolutionary Change for Your Technology Business
**Author**: David J. Anderson  
**Year**: 2010  
**Publisher**: Blue Hole Press  
**Summary**: The definitive text on applying Kanban to software and knowledge work. Covers board design, WIP limits, classes of service, metrics (lead time, cycle time, throughput), and the evolutionary change philosophy. Essential reading for any orchestration role.  
**Key Concepts**: WIP limits, pull system, classes of service, cumulative flow diagrams, cadences.

---

### Scrum: The Art of Doing Twice the Work in Half the Time
**Author**: Jeff Sutherland  
**Year**: 2014  
**Publisher**: Crown Business  
**Summary**: Sutherland presents the origins and core mechanics of Scrum alongside case studies from military, government, and tech organizations. Argues that Scrum's hyperproductivity emerges from small teams, short sprints, and radical transparency.  
**Key Concepts**: Sprint, Sprint Goal, Scrum Master, Product Owner, velocity, impediment removal.

---

### Scrum and XP from the Trenches (2nd Edition)
**Author**: Henrik Kniberg  
**Year**: 2007 (1st ed.), updated 2015  
**Publisher**: InfoQ (free online)  
**Summary**: A practical, honest account of implementing Scrum in a real company. Covers what works, what doesn't, and how to adapt ceremonies to real constraints. One of the most widely-read agile implementation guides.  
**Key Concepts**: Sprint planning in two parts, sprint backlogs, impediment lists, definition of done.

---

### Agile Estimating and Planning
**Author**: Mike Cohn  
**Year**: 2005  
**Publisher**: Prentice Hall  
**Summary**: The authoritative guide on how to estimate software work using story points and planning poker, and how to construct release and iteration plans. Introduces the concept of the velocity-driven sprint plan.  
**Key Concepts**: Story points, planning poker, velocity, release planning, iteration planning, cone of uncertainty.

---

### User Stories Applied: For Agile Software Development
**Author**: Mike Cohn  
**Year**: 2004  
**Publisher**: Addison-Wesley  
**Summary**: Defines what user stories are, how to write them well, and how to use them in planning and backlog management. Introduces the INVEST criteria and acceptance test-driven development at the story level.  
**Key Concepts**: INVEST criteria, acceptance criteria, story splitting, three-part story format (As a / I want / So that).

---

### Personal Kanban: Mapping Work, Navigating Life
**Authors**: Jim Benson & Tonianne DeMaria Barry  
**Year**: 2011  
**Publisher**: Modus Cooperandi Press  
**Summary**: Applies Kanban's two core rules (visualize work, limit WIP) to individuals and small teams. Demonstrates how visualizing cognitive load improves decision-making and reduces overwhelm.  
**Key Concepts**: Value stream mapping for personal work, WIP as cognitive load, kaizen mindset.

---

### Kanban and Scrum: Making the Most of Both
**Authors**: Henrik Kniberg & Mattias Skarin  
**Year**: 2010  
**Publisher**: InfoQ (free online)  
**Summary**: A side-by-side comparison of Scrum and Kanban with practical guidance on when to use each and how to combine them into Scrumban. Valuable for hybrid team setups.  
**Key Concepts**: Scrumban, cadence vs. flow, backlog vs. queue, sprint vs. continuous delivery.

---

### Toyota Production System: Beyond Large-Scale Production
**Author**: Taiichi Ohno  
**Year**: 1988 (English translation)  
**Publisher**: Productivity Press  
**Summary**: The foundational text on Lean thinking. Ohno describes the development of the Toyota Production System, the just-in-time philosophy, and the relentless elimination of waste. The intellectual ancestor of all Kanban and Lean software methods.  
**Key Concepts**: Just-in-time, jidoka, muda (waste), kaizen, pull production, andon cord.
