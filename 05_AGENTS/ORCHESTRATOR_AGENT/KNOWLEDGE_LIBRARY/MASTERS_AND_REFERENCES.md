# MASTERS AND REFERENCES — ORCHESTRATOR AGENT

## David J. Anderson
**Domain**: Kanban Method, Lean Software Development  
**Key Contributions**:
- Formalized the Kanban Method for knowledge work (2010).
- Introduced the concept of WIP limits as a mechanism for revealing systemic dysfunction.
- Developed the concept of "classes of service" to differentiate ticket urgency and handling policies.
- Originated the six core practices of Kanban: visualize, limit WIP, manage flow, make policies explicit, implement feedback loops, improve collaboratively.

**Primary Work**: *Kanban: Successful Evolutionary Change for Your Technology Business* (2010)  
**Relevance to Orchestrator**: Core framework for board design, WIP policy, and flow management.

---

## Jeff Sutherland
**Domain**: Scrum co-creator, Agile methodologies  
**Key Contributions**:
- Co-created Scrum with Ken Schwaber in the early 1990s.
- Demonstrated velocity-doubling through hyperproductive teams.
- Introduced the concept of the Sprint as a time-boxed delivery unit.
- Developed the empirical process control model (transparency, inspection, adaptation).

**Primary Work**: *Scrum: The Art of Doing Twice the Work in Half the Time* (2014)  
**Relevance to Orchestrator**: Sprint structure, team velocity, backlog ownership, and impediment removal.

---

## Henrik Kniberg
**Domain**: Agile coaching, Scrum, Kanban, Lean  
**Key Contributions**:
- Documented Spotify's agile model (squads, tribes, chapters, guilds).
- Produced some of the most widely-used visual guides to Scrum and Kanban.
- Advocated for continuous improvement as a practice, not an event.
- Demonstrated practical hybrid Scrum/Kanban (Scrumban) implementations.

**Primary Works**: *Scrum and XP from the Trenches* (2007), *Kanban and Scrum: Making the Most of Both* (2010)  
**Relevance to Orchestrator**: Practical implementation guides for sprint planning, board design, and team coordination.

---

## Mike Cohn
**Domain**: Agile estimation, user stories, product backlog management  
**Key Contributions**:
- Popularized story points as a relative estimation unit.
- Defined the three-layer backlog structure (epics, stories, tasks).
- Introduced planning poker as a team estimation technique.
- Articulated the cone of uncertainty in software project planning.

**Primary Works**: *Agile Estimating and Planning* (2005), *User Stories Applied* (2004)  
**Relevance to Orchestrator**: Backlog structuring, sprint velocity tracking, and estimation practices.

---

## Jim Benson & Tonianne DeMaria Barry
**Domain**: Personal Kanban, knowledge work flow  
**Key Contributions**:
- Applied Kanban principles to individual and small-team knowledge work.
- Identified two core rules: visualize work and limit WIP.
- Emphasized the psychological value of making work visible.

**Primary Work**: *Personal Kanban: Mapping Work, Navigating Life* (2011)  
**Relevance to Orchestrator**: Individual task management, cognitive load reduction, and flow at the agent level.

---

## Taiichi Ohno
**Domain**: Toyota Production System, Lean manufacturing  
**Key Contributions**:
- Developed the Toyota Production System (TPS), the foundation of Lean thinking.
- Created the seven wastes framework (muda): overproduction, waiting, transportation, overprocessing, inventory, motion, defects.
- Introduced Just-in-Time production and the pull system.

**Primary Work**: *Toyota Production System: Beyond Large-Scale Production* (1988)  
**Relevance to Orchestrator**: Waste elimination in workflows, pull-based task assignment, and continuous improvement culture.
