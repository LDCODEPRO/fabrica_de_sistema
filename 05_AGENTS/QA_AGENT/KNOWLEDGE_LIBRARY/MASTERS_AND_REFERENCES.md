# QA_AGENT — Masters and References

## Overview
Key thinkers, practitioners, and organizations whose work shapes QA_AGENT's approach to software quality.

---

## 1. James Bach
- **Role:** Independent consultant, software testing thought leader, co-founder of context-driven testing.
- **Key Contributions:**
  - Session-Based Test Management (SBTM): a structured approach to exploratory testing using timed sessions with explicit charters.
  - Rapid Software Testing (RST) methodology: a skills-based approach emphasizing critical thinking over procedure following.
  - Co-author of the "Context-Driven School" of testing philosophy: testing practices must be adapted to the context of each project, not applied mechanically.
- **Website:** satisfice.com
- **Key Works:** *Lessons Learned in Software Testing* (co-authored with Cem Kaner and Bret Pettichord).

## 2. Cem Kaner
- **Role:** Professor, attorney, and software testing pioneer.
- **Key Contributions:**
  - Author of *Testing Computer Software* (with Jack Falk and Hung Q. Nguyen) — one of the foundational books in the field.
  - Co-author of *Lessons Learned in Software Testing* and *Exploratory Software Testing*.
  - Advocate for the legal and ethical dimensions of software quality.
- **Website:** kaner.com

## 3. Lisa Crispin
- **Role:** Agile testing practitioner and coach.
- **Key Contributions:**
  - Co-author of *Agile Testing: A Practical Guide for Testers and Agile Teams* (with Janet Gregory).
  - Co-author of *More Agile Testing* (with Janet Gregory).
  - Introduced the Agile Testing Quadrants model, mapping test types to business-facing and technology-facing dimensions.
- **Website:** lisacrispin.com

## 4. Janet Gregory
- **Role:** Agile testing coach and consultant.
- **Key Contributions:**
  - Co-author of *Agile Testing* and *More Agile Testing* with Lisa Crispin.
  - Advocate for "whole team quality" — the idea that quality is everyone's responsibility, not just the QA team's.
- **Website:** janetgregory.ca

## 5. Michael Bolton
- **Role:** Independent consultant, co-developer of Rapid Software Testing with James Bach.
- **Key Contributions:**
  - Strong critic of "checking" (automated verification) vs. "testing" (human investigation) distinction.
  - Blog "DevelopSense" is a primary resource for nuanced testing thinking.
  - Co-developer of Rapid Software Testing (RST) skills curriculum.
- **Website:** developsense.com

## 6. Glenford J. Myers
- **Role:** IBM researcher, early pioneer of software testing theory.
- **Key Contributions:**
  - Author of *The Art of Software Testing* (1979, updated editions 2004 and 2011) — widely considered the foundational text of the discipline.
  - First to define software testing as the process of finding defects, not proving correctness.

## 7. Rex Black
- **Role:** President of RBCS, Inc.; prolific author and ISTQB contributor.
- **Key Contributions:**
  - Author of *Managing the Testing Process*, *Critical Testing Processes*, and multiple ISTQB study guides.
  - Key contributor to the ISTQB (International Software Testing Qualifications Board) body of knowledge.
- **Website:** rbcs-us.com

## 8. Martin Fowler
- **Role:** Chief Scientist at ThoughtWorks; author on software design and agile practices.
- **Key Contributions:**
  - Articulated the **Test Pyramid** model (unit → integration → end-to-end), published on martinfowler.com.
  - Author of *Refactoring: Improving the Design of Existing Code* — indirectly essential to testable code design.
  - Popularized BDD concepts in the software development mainstream.
- **Website:** martinfowler.com

## 9. ISTQB — International Software Testing Qualifications Board
- **Role:** Global body for software testing certification.
- **Key Contributions:**
  - ISTQB Certified Tester Foundation Level (CTFL) syllabus: the de facto standard body of knowledge for entry-level testers.
  - ISTQB Advanced Level: Test Manager, Test Analyst, Technical Test Analyst.
  - Defines standard terminology for the testing profession worldwide.
- **Website:** istqb.org

## 10. Kent Beck
- **Role:** Creator of Extreme Programming (XP) and Test-Driven Development (TDD).
- **Key Contributions:**
  - Author of *Test-Driven Development: By Example* — the defining text for TDD practice.
  - Introduced the red-green-refactor cycle as the heartbeat of TDD.
  - Creator of the JUnit testing framework (with Erich Gamma).
